From Business Management to Computer Science: A Journey of Discovery

December 25, 2024 Personal Growth

The Seeds of Change

My relationship with technology started long before my academic journey. Building custom gaming PCs from scratch wasn't just a hobby; it was my first real taste of understanding how hardware components worked together to create something powerful. These early experiences of selecting parts, troubleshooting issues, and optimizing performance were unknowingly laying the groundwork for my future in computer science.

The Turning Point

Despite having a solid foundation in business management, something was missing. The analytical skills I gained were valuable, but I found myself increasingly drawn to the technical aspects of every project. The moment of clarity came during a business systems analysis course, where I realized I was more interested in how the software worked than in the business processes it supported.

First Steps into Development

My journey into coding began with a simple weather application—a project that would now make me chuckle with its "janky" implementation. But that first experience of writing code, debugging issues, and finally seeing my creation work was transformative. It wasn't just about making something functional; it was about the problem-solving process and the satisfaction of building something from scratch.

From Business Management to Computer Science: A Journey of Discovery

December 25, 2024 Personal Growth

From Classroom to Real World

The true validation of my career switch came during my fall internship, where I developed a CRM application. This project brought together everything I'd been learning—database design, user interface development, and business logic implementation. More importantly, it showed me how my business background gave me a unique perspective in software development. Understanding both the technical requirements and business needs helped me create solutions that weren't just technically sound but also practical and user-focused.

Looking Forward

The transition hasn't been without its challenges. Learning complex programming concepts requires dedication and late nights. However, each successful project, from that first weather app to the CRM system, reinforces that this path aligns perfectly with my passions and skills.

Lessons Learned

1. Don't ignore your natural inclinations—they often point to your true calling
2. Previous experience is never wasted—my business background provides valuable context in software development
3. The best learning comes from building real projects, no matter how simple they start

The journey from business management to computer science has taught me that career paths aren't always linear, and that's okay. Sometimes the longest route leads to the best destination. As I continue pursuing my bachelor's in computer science, I'm excited about combining my business acumen with technical skills to create solutions that make a real difference.
